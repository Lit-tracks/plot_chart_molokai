Plot Chart

  • Beginning
    • • Rachel encounters a ship named Mokoli’i with lepers boarding in it while she waits for her father.
    • • Uncle Pono has been taken by the Board of Health and confined in the Kalihi Receiving Center. Later on, Rachel’s mother Dorothy discovers Rachel’s blemish on her leg, and is surprised to find out Rachel did not feel pain.
    • • Rachel gets taken by the Board of Health and is sent to the Kalihi Receiving Center, similar to her uncle Pono. She grew terrified by the people inside the center, who held visible ailments of leprosy.
    • • Pono leaves for Molokai, as well as Rachel later on. She enters Mokoli’I, the same boat she saw on the harbor while waiting for her father.
  • Middle
    • • Rachel arrives in Kalaupapa, Molokai, and had reunited with her uncle Pono once more, and meets his friend Haleola, a local shaman in the village.
    • • Ambrose and Sister Catherine took Rachel to the Bishop Home, much to her, Pono, and Haleola’s distaste. Although bitter about her new home, she grew accustomed to it and made friends, such as Emily and Violet
    • • Rachel decided to run away from the Bishop Home to find Pono, who had stopped visiting due to his worsening condition. She was able to spend her last few days with him and Haleola before he died. Rachel then requests Haleola if she can be her aunt.
    • • Rachel receives frequent letters from her father, but less frequent from her mother. These letters inform Rachel the details of her family after Rachel’s departure. When Rachel’s father, Henry, comes to visit, he informed her that Dorothy took the children and left him.
    • • Rachel becomes seventeen, becoming more lively than ever even though she has leprosy. She sneaked away from the Bishop Home with her friends and attended a party on a certain part of Molokai.
    • • Rachel gained permission to leave the Bishop home to care of a sick Haleola, later befriending another resident, Lani, who showed her hula
    • • Sister Catherine’s mother committed suicide while Haleola passed away. Rachel remained positive helping a depressed Sister Catherine who could not cope with her loss of her mother.
    • • Rachel experienced prejudice from a boy named Jake who discovered her leprosy. She motivated herself in ridding her leprosy as soon as possible by attending a leprosy investigation center. After viewing Hailey’s comet, she decided to leave and return to Kalaupapa
    • • Emily and Lani passed away due to illness, much to Rachel’s sadness
    • • Rachel met a man by the name of Kenji. They began developing a relationship together and soon became married. They bore a daughter by the name of Ruth, yet must give her up to avoid her catching leprosy.
    • • Rachel and Kenji improved each other’s lives together, as well as the village of Kalaupapa, vowing to stay with each other all the way to the end.
    • • Crossen, a navy sailor, entered Kalaupapa, much to his resentment. Meanwhile Kalaupapa’s residents caught whiffs of an invasion in Oahu from Japanese forces. The village soon fell under martial law.
    • • Rachel and Kenji entered Crossen’s cottage to stop his abuse on a woman named Felicia. Kenji and Crossen both engaged in a brawl, ending with Kenji fatally hurt and Crossen apprehended. Kenji died moments after.
    • • Rachel held a funeral in honor of her husband. She attended Crossen’s trial to testify on behalf of her deceased husband. She is initially skeptical about the trial due to Crossen’s status as a military soldier, but was surprised when he was actually sentenced in years of prison.
    • • During a tsunami, Rachel’s health worsened due to the effects of her leprosy, but after testing a new drug, Promin, she recovered from her ailments and leprosy all together.
    • • On her sixties, Rachel was able to be released from Kalaupapa. She made her farewells to everyone before her voyage.
  • End
    • • Rachel left Molokai after being cleared by her doctors and voyaged back to Oahu, with a motive of reuniting her family once more
    • • Rachel entered a lively Oahu, much different from her past, yet encountered prejudice from residents, even her aunt Florence, who discovered her scars caused by her leprosy
    • • Rachel ventured into Maui after making contact with her sister Sarah via her Maui telephone number and later reunited with Sarah
    • • Rachel discovered that her mother Dorothy took the family into hiding in Maui without her husband Henry’s attention, left Sarah and Ben with a known relative, and took care of Kimo until his death. This shocked Rachel, since she soon realized that all the stories in the letters made from her father were to mask the truth of her family during Rachel’s time in Kalaupapa.
    • • At Oahu, Rachel began searching for Ruth, her daughter. With the help of Sister Catherine, she was able to pinpoint her location in California.
    • • Rachel wrote a letter to be sent to Ruth in hopes of making contact. Later on Rachel and Ruth began calling each other, awkward in the beginning. They planned out a meeting in California, if Rachel can get there.
    • • Rachel travels to California via S.S. Lurline, and reunited with Ruth once more. She discovered that even after sending Ruth off to avoid her catching leprosy in Molokai, Ruth did not enjoy a happy life due to her and her adoptive family being forced into an internment camp for the Japanese people during the war, much to Rachel’s disappointment on herself.
    • • After returning to Hawaii, Rachel moved in with her sister Sarah on Maui, where she will live the rest of her life.
    • • Many years later, Ruth and her daughter Peggy visits Kalaupapa, Molokai, to attend her mother’s funeral. She sees the impact Rachel had made to the village, as well as her adventures around the world through her souvenirs.
